Urban Sprawl Monitoring

The "Urban Sprawl Monitoring" project utilizes advanced technologies to analyze urban expansion patterns. By capturing and assessing spatial and temporal data, this project provides valuable insights into the dynamics of urban growth. Its aim is to support informed decision-making for sustainable urban planning and development. Through comprehensive monitoring, this project contributes to creating resilient and livable cities for the future.

Feature detection using Opencv and Numpy

The "Overhead Tank Detection" project utilizes OpenCV and NumPy to automate the detection of overhead tanks from aerial photography. By leveraging computer vision techniques and advanced algorithms, it enables efficient and accurate identification and mapping of these tanks. This project plays a vital role in urban planning and water resource management, supporting sustainable infrastructure development.

Gaja Cyclone Mapping and Recovery

The "Gaja Cyclone Mapping and Recovery" project assesses the damage caused by Cyclone Gaja and facilitates the recovery process. Through advanced mapping technologies and data analysis, it provides crucial insights into the affected areas. The project aims to support targeted strategies for rehabilitation, efficient resource allocation, and the restoration of cyclone-impacted regions. By working collaboratively, this project helps communities rebuild and enhances their resilience in the face of future disasters.

The "Water Distribution Network for Thoothukudi City" project proposes an advanced system to address water scarcity and promote efficient resource management. By leveraging innovative technologies, the project aims to optimize water distribution, minimize losses, and encourage water-saving measures. Forest Fire Risk Zone Mapping

The "Forest Fire Risk Zone Mapping" project analyzes spatial data to identify high-risk areas for forest fires in Brasilia and Kangaroo Island during May 2019 and December 2019, respectively. By employing advanced mapping techniques, this project aims to provide valuable insights for proactive fire management. The findings will assist authorities in implementing preventive measures, allocating resources, and safeguarding these regions from the destructive impacts of forest fires.

Land Sustainable Mapping for Dumpyard Site

The "Land Sustainable Mapping for Dumpyard Site" project utilizes spatial analysis techniques to assess land suitability for dumpyard sites. By considering factors such as terrain, proximity to populated areas, and ecological sensitivity, this project generates sustainable land maps. These maps enable informed decision-making, facilitating efficient waste disposal while minimizing environmental impacts.

During the sixth semester of my studies, I had the opportunity to participate in a week-long surveying camp where we extensively learned the fundamental principles of surveying. Throughout the camp, we were introduced to various aspects such as distance and angle measurements, calculations, error correction, as well as higher-order surveying and satellite navigation concepts. This practical surveying education equipped us with the skills and confidence to work with precision and accuracy in industrial domains in the future. Working in small groups, we had the privilege to practice alongside skilled surveyors and utilize a range of surveying instruments. Furthermore, we learned how to upload point data from the field into AutoCAD and used it to create topographic and road maps, including the creation of a contour map as part of our surveying exercises.

PROJECt poster

As an active participant in Terzaghi'19, a prestigious national-level symposium, I had the opportunity to create a visually captivating and informative poster for the expo. Titled "The Role of Mangroves in Natural Calamities," my poster aimed to highlight the vital significance of mangroves in mitigating the impacts of natural disasters and calamities. Through meticulous research and a strategic design approach, I presented the multifaceted role that mangroves play in safeguarding coastal ecosystems and communities. The poster incorporated engaging visuals, concise yet impactful text, and a carefully chosen color scheme to convey the importance of mangroves as a natural defense mechanism. This elaborate and professional creation holds a special place in my portfolio, reflecting my dedication to environmental science and showcasing my ability to effectively communicate complex concepts in a visually appealing manner.

Geo-informatics Student Organization

In 2019, I led a team in developing a curriculum for Geoinformatics and Civil students at a Surveying and Mapping Camp. We focused on teaching surveying techniques and creating visually appealing story maps using advanced technologies. Our goal was to enhance spatial comprehension and foster creativity by merging art and Geoinformatics. The program provided students with a platform to explore the intersection of art and geoinformatics, improving their spatial reasoning and communication skills through captivating maps.
